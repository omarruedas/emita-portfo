Objective 1

Integrate the knowledge, theories and methods of a variety of disciplines to inform decision making.

It is the nurses responsibility to ensure the patient understands their plan of care. Integrating various disciplines in the patient's care assists the client in making an informed decision in their health. The nurse must assess the patient and integrate their knowledge, theories, and methods that they have learned throughout their nursing career. In Pharmacology, NURS- 2860 we wrote Drugs at the Bedside paper which helped me better understand the concept of informed decision making. The paper depicts a hypothetical patient and their health prognosis and the steps to safely administer medication to the patient. The nurse must critically think and view the patients health holistically when administering a medication. The paper discusses that it is the nurses responsibility to ensure that the patient understands why they are taking their medication. The nurse is also responsible in ensuring that the patient knows how to properly take their medication.

Objective 2

Participate in the process of inquiry, analysis, and information literacy to promote quality care and patient safety.

The use of evidence based practice (EBP) ensures that the most up to date information is used while providing care for the patient. The patient's safety is the number one priority. The nurse must be knowledgeable in finding evidence based practice and utilizing the EBP during the patient's time of care. The use of the most current practice is vital in ensuring the best patient outcome. An assignment that assisted me to connect the use of EBP to promote quality care and patient safety was my Mental Health Case Study for NURS-3910. I used a plethora of peer-reviewed articles to find evidence for planned interventions for each nursing diagnosis. Many of the articles provided examples of how to approach a patient based off of their diagnosis. This assignment seamlessly connected the importance of the use of information literacy to promote quality and patient safety.

Objective 8

Patient centered care requires a holistic approach. There are many different values and practices that come along with different cultures. The nurse will need to assess what the patient's preferences are and include the preferences in the patient's plan of care. Understanding the various factors that make up a patient's attitudes and beliefs helps the nurse create realistic goals for the patient. The family assessment paper for Community Health Clinical, NURS-4410 was an in depth assignment primarily focusing on the patient and families environment, values, and attitudes. Gathering such information assisted in a diagnosis, goal, objective, intervention, and evaluation of the patient's care. Understanding where the patient comes from and involving patient preferences allows for better patient-centered care.

Objective 11

Implement holistic, patient-centered care that reflects an understanding of human growth and development, pathophysiology, pharmacology, medical and nursing management across the health/illness continuum, across the lifespan, and in all healthcare settings.

Each patient has their own understanding of what health means to them. Some patients may seek acute care whereas others prefer comfort care depending on the patient's diagnosis. . There are many different ways to help treat a patient such as the use of pharmacology. Holistic care means that the nurse is tending to the patient's overall wellness rather than just treating the illness. The healthcare team can provide various types of complementary therapies to the patient. In Pediatrics Nursing, NURS-3800 we created a powerpoint presentation for parents of cancer children. The powerpoint includes complementary therapies catered towards children. For example, we included teaching on using aromatherapy. Different scents can assist with the side effects of cancer treatments. The use of complementary therapies implement a holistic approach when caring for various patient populations.
